# S16E05 "The King's Gambit" — Fast Pass Episode Pitch

## Logline
Crowley recruits Sam and Dean to investigate a collapse in Hell's command chain, only to discover an ancient locked stratum beneath the known circles where something has opened a door that should never open.

## Tone
- Supernatural noir with heist momentum
- Dean/Crowley buddy-chaos energy
- Claustrophobic underworld suspense

## A-Story (Dean + Crowley)
- Demons are deserting Hell in organized waves.
- Crowley cannot trust his own lieutenants, so he turns to Dean as an "outside auditor."
- The trail leads to old freight tunnels beneath Hell's ninth ring.
- Dean and Crowley stage a break-in to a records vault and learn there are pre-Lucifer sectors.

## B-Story (Sam + Cas)
- Sam and Cas track surface-side omens tied to sudden demonic silence.
- They uncover ritual signatures older than angelic catalogues.
- Their findings map to a hidden gate below Hell.

## Key Set Pieces
- Cold open: demon platoon abandons a fortified post in terror.
- Dean/Crowley infiltration of infernal bureaucracy hub.
- Elevator descent past "Floor 9" into unlisted levels.
- Final reveal: one ancient door is already open.

## Character Engine
- Dean and Crowley operate like rivals who function best under pressure.
- Sam balances tactical caution with urgency.
- Cas is unsettled by divine blind spots in the archive.

## Ending Button
Crowley, shaken and trying not to show it: "I've ruled Hell for centuries and never went below the ninth floor. Now I know why."

## Why This Episode Works
- Launches a season-level threat from a contained genre engine.
- Uses fan-favorite Crowley dynamic while escalating mythology.
- Ends with a clean hook into episode 6.
