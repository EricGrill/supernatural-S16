# S16E11: Salt and Iron

## Logline
A routine ghost case collapses into a survival horror night when Sam and Dean discover the hunter rulebook is failing in real time: salt breaks, iron burns out, and every old defense is becoming temporary.

## Why This Episode Exists
- Pushes the season mythology into practical horror: the boundary damage is no longer abstract.
- Re-centers classic road-hunt tone while proving the world itself is destabilizing.
- Gives Sam the thesis line: the threat is systemic erosion, not one monster.

## A-Story (Monster-of-the-Week)
- The brothers roll into a small mill town after a family is wiped out in a "locked room" haunting.
- The spirit is a dockworker murdered in 1911; normally straightforward: salt lines, iron restraints, burn the remains.
- Each tactic works briefly, then fails hard: salt line smears itself, iron chain heats and snaps, EMF and sigils flicker.
- The ghost learns faster than expected, becoming more physical and aggressive each encounter.

## B-Story (Myth Arc)
- Jane arrives mid-case after tracking similar "rule-failure" incidents in three states.
- She confirms a broader pattern: boundaries between domains are thinning unevenly.
- Her line to Sam: "The walls between things are thinning. All of them."
- Sam realizes hunters will lose the war of attrition if tools keep degrading.

## Core Horror Set Pieces
- Motel room siege where a perfect salt ring decays grain by grain.
- Iron-nailed panic room turning into a pressure cooker as nails rust in minutes.
- Church basement exorcism interrupted when Latin wards burn off the walls.
- Final chase in the old mill with unstable reality pockets and flickering gravity.

## Climax
- Instead of relying on a single banishment ritual, Sam and Dean layer multiple imperfect methods (salt + iron + fire + spoken rite) in sequence.
- The ghost is neutralized for now, but only through improvisation and constant adaptation.
- Victory feels expensive and temporary, not clean.

## Ending Button
- Back at the bunker, Sam logs a new hunter protocol: "No single defense can be trusted alone."
- Dean quietly replaces their trunk kits with redundant backups.
- Jane pins a map of "rule-failure hotspots" on the table; it is spreading faster than they thought.

## Tone / Production Notes (Fast Pass)
- Lean scary-horror episode with practical effects and tight night exteriors.
- Keep humor minimal; dread and urgency carry the hour.
- Visual motif: familiar protective symbols failing on camera.
