# S16E07 "Starovjerstvo" — Fast Pass Episode Pitch

## Logline
A dual-timeline case reveals Jane's bloodline of Serbian witches and the oldest truth in the season: creation was built around a hungry absence that lives in the seams of reality.

## Tone
- Folk-horror mythology episode
- History-and-present parallel suspense
- Heavy lore with emotional family core

## A-Story (Present Day: Sam + Dean + Jane)
- Jane finally discloses her family history after seal failures accelerate.
- Sam and Dean help her investigate ritual breaches connected to Balkan ward symbols.
- They discover the current attacks are not demonic and not angelic.
- A surviving ward map points to "edge sites" where reality is thinnest.

## B-Story (1347 Serbia Timeline)
- Jane's ancestor line is introduced in plague-era village panic.
- Her grandmother's predecessor performs the first binding rite at enormous personal cost.
- The rite does not defeat the threat; it only forces boundaries to hold.
- Family doctrine is established: guard the edges, never name the void directly.

## Key Set Pieces
- Cold open: a church wardline peels off stone like burned paint.
- Intercut ritual sequence: 1347 binding mirrored with present-day reactivation.
- Bunker lore-wall scene where Sam links the Empty to a deeper pre-creation absence.
- Final chapel confrontation where Jane completes the seal but one gap remains active.

## Character Engine
- Jane moves from guarded ally to season-critical protagonist.
- Sam becomes the lore integrator, turning scattered clues into a coherent threat model.
- Dean grounds the mythology in practical stakes and immediate action.

## Ending Button
Jane (quiet, shaken): "The Empty is where things go to die. This was here before death had a place to go."

## Why This Episode Works
- Delivers core season mythology in a dramatic, character-led way.
- Expands scope without losing episode-level momentum.
- Sets up episode 8 with clear threat escalation and unresolved edge breach.
