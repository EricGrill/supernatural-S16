SUPERNATURAL
Season 16, Episode 3
"The Cartographer"
Speed-Pass Episode Pitch (Workflow Test)

LOGLINE
Sam, Dean, and Cas descend into a sealed 1919 Men of Letters outpost where a self-drawing map tracks ley lines that are shifting in real time. As the tunnels rearrange and old warding fails, Sam’s pattern analysis becomes the only way out.

COLD OPEN
1919. A Men of Letters survey team marks “stable” lines under a Midwestern outpost. Lights fail. One by one, the team vanishes without signs of struggle. Last survivor seals the vault and writes: "The lines moved."

ACT 1
Present day. Sam ties recent cattle drops, disappearances, and historical disaster dates into one moving geometric pattern. Dean wants to burn the nest and move on. Cas confirms angelic records skip this area entirely. They locate the buried outpost entrance under a collapsed church root cellar.

ACT 2
Inside the bunker: dead radios, mechanical doors, map room intact. The main table map starts drawing new lines on its own in black ink that appears from dry paper. The map marks the brothers’ current position and predicts a corridor collapse two minutes before it happens. They realize the place isn’t haunted in the classic sense; it is being re-routed by something “between” known ley paths.

ACT 3
Claustrophobic descent. Branches of the tunnel system physically shift. Dean gets separated in a false corridor loop. Cas burns grace trying to phase through walls and fails. Sam notices each movement follows a mathematical cadence tied to old eclipse intervals. He uses the cadence to call safe windows over radio static while Dean fights off blind, heat-seeking wraith-like entities that disappear when directly seen.

ACT 4
They reach the sealed lower archive. A final ledger reveals prior outbreaks were misfiled as famine/plague to avoid panic. The self-drawing map marks one line heading toward Sioux Falls and another to Lebanon. Jack answers a prayer-line briefly: "I can see the stars. I still can't see the dark between these lines."

CLIMAX
Sam overlays old map vellum with present coordinates and identifies a temporary anchor point. Dean and Cas hold off the entities while Sam redraws a corrected path circle in blood-and-ink on the live map table. The tunnel stabilizes for ninety seconds, enough to escape and reseal.

TAG
Back at the bunker, Sam pins the moving-pattern board as an ongoing threat stream, not a one-off monster case. Dean admits Sam saved all of them. Final beat: the map table, now stored in a locked room, scratches out one new line on its own.

CHARACTER FOCUS
- Sam: lead strategist, pattern recognition under pressure, emotional confidence as primary problem-solver.
- Dean: tactical pressure and trust shift toward Sam’s calls.
- Cas: limits exposed; power no longer a guaranteed fix.

MYTH ARC FUNCTION
- Confirms post-Chuck world has blind zones even for Jack.
- Introduces moving old-line system tied to season-long threat.
- Seeds future targets (Sioux Falls, Lebanon) for connected episodes.

END
